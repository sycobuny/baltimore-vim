Suggest a Talk

If you're looking to Submit a Talk and just need some fuel to kickstart the fire, click here to jump straight to the "Suggested Talks" section for ideas which have previously been submitted.

Otherwise, please use the form below if there's something you'd like to hear about (you can also send a tweet to @baltimorevim on Twitter with your idea):

Suggested Talks

  • Accessibility in Vim - Requested by Stephen Belcher

    Accessibility is an important issue in designing systems to maximize use for everyone, but the tools we use to design those systems are sometimes overlooked. I'm curious whether there has been work done to ensure or enable Vim to be used in an accessible way, and how this would work in variousc accessibility scenarios.

    Give this talk!

  • Emacs for the Vim User - Requested by Stephen Belcher

    Emacs is pretty similar to Vim in a lot of important ways, and is often seen as a competitor. A few plugins, such as Vim-Slime, attempt to mimic functionality therein. But, I've never known much beyond that and I'd like to! If there's someone out there who knows enough about Emacs and Vim who can share how to get the most out of both, I'd love to hear it.

    Give this talk!
